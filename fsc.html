Foreign Studies College (FSC)

Foreign Studies College (FSC) was established on September 15, 1999, whose origin dates back to Department of English and Department of Russian of Northeastern University (NEU) founded in 1923. In 1949, Section of Foreign Language Teaching and Research was created, in September 1984, Division of Foreign Languages was founded, and Department of Foreign Languages was established in 1993, when only undergraduates majoring in English were enrolled.

At present, FSC consists of 4 departments that offer bachelor's degree, namely Department of English, Department of Japanese, Department of Russian, and Department of German; and 4 foreign language teaching and research divisions, namely Division One of College English Teaching and Research, Division Two of College English Teaching and Research, Division of Postgraduate English Teaching and Research, and Division of French Teaching and Research. FSC has one primary master degree discipline, namely Foreign Language and Literature, with 6 affiliated master degree programs, which include Foreign Linguistics and Applied Linguistics (i.e. English, Japanese), English Language and Literature, Japanese Language and Literature, Russian Language and Literature, Master of Translation and Interpreting (MTI). In addition, FSC has 8 research institutes, including Institute of American Culture, Institute of Language Education, Institute of Contrastive Research of Chinese and Japanese Cultures, Institute of Applied Linguistics, Institute of Science, Technology and Culture, Institute of Contrastive Linguistics, and Institute of Russian Language and Culture.

FSC has 158 faculty and staff members, 138 of whom are full-time teachers. There are 10 professors and 46 associate professors. FSC has 1 member (Vice Chairman) of National College English Education Steering Committee, Ministry of Education, 1 hundred-level laureate of "Talent Project of Liaoning Province," 2 "Provincial Level Outstanding Teachers" of Liaoning Province, and 4 "Provincial Level Key Teachers" of Liaoning Province. FSC has nearly 20 foreign teachers.

FSC has so far made many kinds of achievement in such areas as foreign languages teaching reform, computer assisted language teaching, corpus linguistics, comparative study of Chinese and Japanese cultures, and American studies. FSC has been awarded 1 second prize of "National Level Teaching Achievement Prizes," 1 "National Quality Course," 2 "Provincial Quality Courses," 1 "Provincial Quality Demonstration Course," and 10 plus "Provincial Level Teaching Achievement Prizes." Teachers of FSC has published nearly 100 "Nationally Planned Textbooks of Ninth Five-Year Plan, Tenth Five-Year Plan, and Eleventh Five-Year Plan" and undertaken more than 30 research projects of both provincial and national levels, one of which is "National Key Project of Humanities and Social Sciences." FSC has been approved as a Demonstration Base for University English Teaching Reform by the Ministry of Education.

FSC has established relationship of academic exchanges and cooperation programs with over 40 higher education institutions in such countries and regions as United States of America, England, Canada, Germany, France, Japan, Russia, Republic of Korea, Hong Kong, and Taiwan. Every year, nearly 30% of the undergraduates participate in exchange programs in foreign countries.

Since its founding, FSC has set it goal of "cultivating quality, versatile talents of solid basic language skills, good command of foreign languages, and global perspectives." Oriented to the world, FSC has developed a multi-level education mode of foreign languages, with emphasis on fostering students' global vision, overall quality, and foreign languages competence.

In the past 3 years, over 30 and 50 students won national and provincial awards in various foreign languages competitions respectively. For 3 consecutive years, the employment rate of graduates reaches 95%. Of all the graduates, 30% go to domestic graduate schools and 10% go abroad for further studies.

In the future, FSC will continuously take its mission of "Promoting Eastern and Western Culture and Cultivating Global-Oriented Talents" against the majestic background of NEU's multi-discipline advantages. Encouraging moral senses and study culture, assimilating new and advanced concepts, and promoting humanity and cultivation, FSC will endeavor to build an international, teaching-research-and-service-oriented college with characteristic disciplines and first-class teaching strength.
